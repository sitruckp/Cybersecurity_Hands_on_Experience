# Security Operations Center (SOC) Policies and Procedures Manual

**Version:** 1.0-AR (Audit-Ready)  
**Prepared by:** Kevin P. Curtis  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Classification:** Confidential – Internal Use Only

## 1. Introduction & Purpose
The SOC monitors, detects, analyzes, responds to, and supports recovery from cybersecurity events.  

**Framework Alignment:** NIST CSF 2.0 (Govern, Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover).

## 2. SOC Governance (NIST CSF 2.0 – Govern)
- Roles & Responsibilities defined
- Authority model: SOC recommends; Server Operations executes disruptive actions
- Annual policy review

## 3. Asset & Risk Identification (Identify)
- Monthly asset inventory
- Quarterly vulnerability assessments
- Criticality ranking

## 4. Protection Controls (Protect)
- Least privilege for SOC tools
- MFA required
- Awareness training

## 5. Detection & Continuous Monitoring (Detect)
- Centralized SIEM with observe-first deployment
- Defined alert triage SLAs
- Severity classification matrix

## 6. Incident Response (Respond)
- NIST-aligned lifecycle
- Detailed severity matrix and escalation
- Playbook usage

## 7. Evidence Handling & Forensics
- Read-only logs where possible
- Chain-of-custody logging
- Forensically sound methods

## 8. Recovery (Recover)
- Validated backups
- Post-recovery monitoring
- Formal after-action reviews

## 9. Metrics, Reporting & Continuous Improvement
- MTTD, MTTR, MTTE, false-positive rate
- Non-punitive metrics

## 10. Detection Engineering Lifecycle
1. Identify need  
2. Develop rule  
3. Test in observe-only  
4. Deploy  
5. Tune  
6. Retire

## 11. References
- NIST CSF 2.0, NIST SP 800-61 Rev. 2, NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5, CIS Benchmarks

**Audit-Readiness Summary:** Demonstrates governance, aligns with standards, and supports insurance and regulatory reviews.
