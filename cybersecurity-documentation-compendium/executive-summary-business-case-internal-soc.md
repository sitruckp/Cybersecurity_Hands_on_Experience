# Executive Summary / Business Case – Transitioning to an Internal Security Operations Center (SOC)

**Prepared by:** Kevin P. Curtis  
**Date:** April 17, 2026

## Purpose
This document outlines the rationale and high-level approach for transitioning key cybersecurity monitoring and response functions to an internal SOC. The goal is to strengthen protection of student information, research data, and operational systems while moving toward a sustainable hybrid model.

## Current Environment and Drivers
- Elevated sector risk (ransomware, data theft, credential attacks)
- Operational complexity in a higher-education environment
- Regulatory expectations (FERPA, GLBA, New Jersey breach notification laws)
- Need for governance maturity and institutional knowledge retention

## Benefits of an Internal SOC Capability

| Benefit                    | Description |
|----------------------------|-----------|
| Context-Aware Response     | Internal analysts with university familiarity can assess threats more accurately. |
| Institutional Knowledge Retention | Lessons learned stay inside the organization. |
| Operational Resilience     | Better coordination during incidents. |
| Compliance and Audit Readiness | Supports regulatory requirements and insurance positioning. |
| Cost Predictability        | Stabilizes long-term expenses. |
| Staff Development          | Creates career pathways and retention. |

## High-Level Phased Approach

**Phase 1: Foundation (Current – 90 Days)**
- Finalize SOC Charter and policies
- Implement Rules of Engagement (RoE)
- Build observe-first Blue Team capabilities
- Maintain outsourced MDR/SOC for 24/7 coverage during transition

**Phase 2: Expansion (90 – 180 Days)**
- Knowledge transfer from MDR provider
- Define RACI matrix
- Limited Purple Team exercises
- Begin metrics tracking

**Phase 3: Maturity (180+ Days)**
- Transition primary monitoring responsibilities internally
- Refine automation and processes
- Evaluate performance using MTTD/MTTR and other metrics

## Strategic Considerations
- Close collaboration with Server Operations, Legal/Compliance, and training teams
- Leverage existing tools and licenses
- Form a cross-functional working group for oversight

## Recommendation
Formally establish a cross-functional working group to refine resourcing, staffing, budget, and oversee the phased transition. This hybrid model represents a proactive investment in the University’s long-term resilience.
